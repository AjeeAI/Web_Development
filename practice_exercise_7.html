Section 1: Understanding IoT Security

IoT security refers to the measures taken to protect connected devices and networks from malicious attacks. Unlike traditional IT systems, IoT devices often have limited processing power, memory, and built-in protection. This makes them prime targets for hackers who exploit vulnerabilities to gain access to larger networks.

Section 2: Major Challenges

Device Proliferation: The more devices there are, the larger the attack surface becomes. Weak Authentication: Many IoT devices ship with default passwords that users never change. Data Privacy Risks: Sensitive information transmitted across networks can be intercepted if not encrypted. Firmware Updates: Some devices lack mechanisms for over-the-air updates, leaving them exposed.
